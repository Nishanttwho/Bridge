1) Automatic trend identification (what your code/chart should check)

Uptrend = price making higher highs and higher lows.
Quick automated checks you can use:

Require the last two pivot lows to be higher than the two before them, OR

Require price > a medium MA (e.g. 50 EMA) and that MA is sloping up.

Downtrend = opposite (lower highs & lower lows, or price < MA and MA sloping down).

Use the pivot test + MA together for stronger filtering: both true → trend confirmed.

2) What to look for on the screenshots (left image = ideal, right = caution)

Left image (green trade box):

You can see a small internal low formed, price moved up, then later dipped below that small low (liquidation).

After that liquidation, price moved up to a swing high.

Fibonacci was drawn from the liquidation low → swing high.

Price retraced into the 0.705–0.69 zone (the red/green band).

A bullish candle touched the zone and closed above the zone low → entry accepted.

Price then moved back up to the swing high → TP hit (green area = trade distance).

Right image (be careful):

Price touched the fib zone but the price action afterwards produced a strong bearish move.

If the candle that touched the zone had closed below the zone low (or closed bearish without strength), that would be no-trade or a losing trade.

This illustrates the rule: wick touch allowed, but close must be bullish (for buys) and not below zone low.

3) Entry logic — step by step (exact, human form)

Assume Uptrend (mirror for downtrend):

Detect a small pivot low (an internal low inside the uptrend).

Wait until price takes out (liquidates) that small pivot low (price makes a new low below it).

After liquidation, wait for the next swing high to form (this is the top of the immediate rally after the liquidation).

You do NOT require that this high breaks the previous larger high. That doesn’t matter.

Draw Fibonacci from the liquidation low (bottom) up to the swing high (top).

Define the buy zone between 70.5% (0.705) and 69% (0.69) retracement of that range.

Show the zone as a filled rectangle on the chart.

Wait for price to tap the zone.

A wick can enter the zone (wick touches allowed).

But the candle that touches the zone must close bullish and must not close below the lower edge (0.69).

Enter long at the close of that bullish candle.

Stop Loss (SL): put SL just below the low of the entry candle (optionally minus a tick buffer).

Take Profit (TP): target the swing high used for the Fibonacci.

If any structural rule breaks (a new lower low for buys), cancel the setup and reset.

4) Quick checklist to mark a valid BUY on the chart

 Trend = up (MA or pivot lows rising)

 Small pivot low identified

 That small low was liquidated (price went below it)

 After liquidation price made a swing high

 Fib drawn from liquidation low → swing high

 Price taps 0.705–0.69 zone

 Candle that tapped zone closed bullish and close ≥ zoneLow

 No new lower low has formed since setup
If all true → enter long.

5) Extra rules / best practices (reduce false signals)

Don’t trade if the candle that tapped the zone is tiny or indecisive — prefer a clear bullish body (optionally set a minimum body %).

Optionally require higher volume (or larger range) on the bullish candle for stronger confirmation.

Prefer setups where the trend filter is confirmed by higher timeframe (e.g., 1-hour shows uptrend while trading 15m).

Use a small tick offset for SL to avoid being stopped by micro-noise.

6) Example mapping to your visuals (language you can paste into code comments)

liquidationLow := the bar.low when price first traded below the earlier pivot low.

swingHigh := the pivot high formed after the liquidation low.

fibHigh := 0.705 level = liquidationLow + (swingHigh - liquidationLow) * 0.705.

fibLow := 0.69 level = liquidationLow + (swingHigh - liquidationLow) * 0.69.

zoneTouched := current bar intersects [fibLow, fibHigh] (wick allowed).

entryCondition := zoneTouched AND bar.close >= fibLow AND bar.close > bar.open AND trend_is_up.

SL := bar.low - min_tick_buffer.

TP := swingHigh.
