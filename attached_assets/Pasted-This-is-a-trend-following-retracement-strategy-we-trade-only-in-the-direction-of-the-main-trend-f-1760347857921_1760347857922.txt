This is a trend-following retracement strategy —
we trade only in the direction of the main trend (for example, uptrend = only buy setups).

🔹 Step-by-Step (For BUY / Uptrend)

Market must be in an uptrend.
That means the market is generally making higher highs and higher lows.

When the price moves up, it will make:

a high,

then a low (retracement),

then another move up (a smaller high).
→ We don’t care if this smaller high breaks the previous one or not.

After that, price will come down again and take out (liquidate) the previous small low it made earlier.
This means it went a bit lower to clean stop losses — that’s okay and expected.

Once that small low is taken, the price then moves up again — this shows the trend is continuing and the market just created a new swing low (the important one we’ll use).

Now, after that upward move, we draw our Fibonacci retracement from:

the swing low (the one that just formed after liquidation)

to the new swing high (the recent top before retracement)

Wait for price to retrace down to the Fibonacci zone between 0.705 and 0.69.

This is our buy zone.

Price must tap this zone,
not close below it,
and the candle that touches it must close bullish (green).

Once that bullish candle forms inside that zone →
✅ We take a Buy trade.

Stop Loss (SL): just below that bullish candle.
Take Profit (TP): the previous swing high.

🔹 Important Notes

We don’t care if the previous high is broken or not — that doesn’t matter in this system.

What matters is:

The structure of higher lows (uptrend).

A liquidation of the small low (internal low).

Then a retracement back to 0.705–0.69 zone with a bullish close.

The same logic works for sell setups, just opposite:

In downtrend (lower highs and lower lows),

Price liquidates a small high,

Retraces up to the 0.705–0.69 zone (from top to bottom),

Closes bearish → take sell.

🔹 In one simple line:

“In an uptrend, after price takes out a small low and goes up again, wait for it to retrace to the 0.705–0.69 zone, reject it bullishly, and buy with target at the last high.”
