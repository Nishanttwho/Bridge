ICT OTE — explained simply (quick & usable)

Think of OTE (Optimal Trade Entry) as “buying the best part of a retracement inside a trend.”
It uses Fibonacci retracement levels inside a clear swing (the dealing range) and waits for price to pull back into a sweet zone where probability favors continuation.

The idea — in one sentence

When the market makes a clear swing (low → high in an uptrend), wait for the retrace into the OTE zone (usually 62%–79%). If the price rejects that zone and shows confirmation, you enter in the direction of the trend with tight stop-loss and target the swing top.

Step-by-step (bullish / uptrend)

Confirm the trend — price above a rising EMA or higher highs & higher lows.

Identify the dealing range: the recent swing LOW → HIGH (the move you want to trade the continuation of).

Draw Fibonacci from that LOW up to that HIGH.

OTE zone = the band between 0.62 (62%) and 0.79 (79%) of that range (some traders prefer the tighter 0.705–0.69 zone).

Wait for price to retrace down into that zone (wicks may touch).

Entry rule: enter when the candle that touched the zone closes bullish (and — your stricter rule — does not close below the zone bottom).

Stop Loss (SL): place just below the entry candle low (small buffer/tick).

Take Profit (TP): first target is the swing high you used for the Fib; you can scale or use extension targets (-50%, -100%) for further targets.

(For a sell in a downtrend, flip everything top ↔ bottom and use bearish closes at the zone.)

Why it works (simple)

The 62–79% area is where institutional orders and smart-money often re-enter a trend after a corrective move.

Trading there gives a better risk-to-reward (smaller SL, clear TP at the swing high).

Requiring a candle close in the trade direction reduces false signals from mere wicks.

Practical entry checklist (copy-paste)

 Trend confirmed (EMA or structure)

 Identify LOW → HIGH dealing range

 Fibonacci drawn low→high

 Price retraces into OTE zone (62–79) or narrow 0.705–0.69 if you prefer

 Candle touches zone (wick OK)

 Candle closes in direction and doesn’t close beyond the zone edge

 No fresh structure break (no new lower low for buys)
→ Enter. SL below candle low. TP at swing high.
