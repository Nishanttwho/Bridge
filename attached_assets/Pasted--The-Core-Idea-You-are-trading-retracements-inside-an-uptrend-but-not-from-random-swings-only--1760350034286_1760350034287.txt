🧠 The Core Idea

You are trading retracements inside an uptrend,
but not from random swings — only from a specific internal structure where the market first liquidates a small low, then continues the uptrend.

You’re not focused on whether the previous high breaks or not.
Your focus is always on what happens to the lows (small internal swing lows).

🔹 Step-by-Step (for a BUY setup)

Market is moving upward (uptrend)
Price is making a series of ups and downs — higher highs and higher lows.
That’s our background condition.

It makes a high → then comes down → forms a small low.
This is just a normal retracement inside the uptrend.

Then the market goes up again and makes another high.
⚠️ You don’t care if this high breaks the previous one or not — it doesn’t matter.
What matters is what happens next.

Now price drops again — this time, it takes out (liquidates) the small low that it made earlier.
In other words, it dips just below that earlier low — hitting the liquidity (stop-losses under that low).

After taking out that low, price goes up again.
That new upward push confirms that the liquidation is complete, and a new valid swing low has been formed.
That swing low (the one that took liquidity) is the important base for your next Fibonacci.

Now you draw your Fibonacci retracement:

From the liquidation low (the new swing low)

Up to the latest high that price just made.

Wait for price to retrace back down into the Fibonacci zone between 0.705 and 0.69.
That’s your buy zone.

Check the candle that touches the zone:

It must not close below the lower end of the zone (0.69).

It should close bullish (green candle).
That means the zone is holding and buyers are stepping in.

✅ When that happens — you buy.

Stop loss: just below that bullish candle.

Take profit: at the previous high.

🔹 In simpler story form

Think of it like this:

The market is going up, then makes a small pullback (a tiny low).
It goes up again but doesn’t need to make a new high.
Then it dips again and sweeps that small low (liquidation).
After that sweep, it rises again — showing that buyers are still strong.
You mark your Fibonacci from the lowest point of that sweep up to the new high.
When it pulls back to the 0.705–0.69 zone, and you see a bullish candle —
You enter the buy.
Target the last high.

🔹 For SELL setups

It’s just the opposite:

Market is in a downtrend.

It makes a low → small high → another low (we don’t care if it breaks or not).

Then it goes up and takes out the small high (liquidation of the high).

Then it drops again — that confirms the downtrend continuation.

You draw the Fibonacci from the liquidation high down to the latest low.

Wait for retracement to the 0.705–0.69 zone (this time, above current price).

When price taps the zone and closes bearish, you sell.

SL above candle, TP at last low.

🔹 Summary in one simple line:

“In an uptrend, wait for the market to take out a small internal low, then go up again — draw Fib from that low to the next high, and buy when price retraces to the 0.705–0.69 zone with a bullish close. Stop below candle, target the high.”
